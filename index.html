Publications

  1. Xie, T.*, Huang, Y. J.*, & Chen, W. F. (2025). Multi-dimensional assignment model and its algorithm for multi-features decision-making problems. Expert Systems with Applications, 270, 126369. DOI: 10.1016/j.eswa.2024.126369 (Q1 TOP SCI, IF:7.5)
    2. This paper proposes a multi-dimensional assignment model (MAM) to address complex decision-making problems involving multi-agents, multi-stages, multi-resources, and multi-constraints, which can effectively describe assignment scenarios ranging from 2D to N-dimensional. To solve MAM, a dimensionality-reducible "Virtual Matching Algorithm" (VMA) is developed by integrating ideas from Roulette-wheel selection, Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO), and Genetic Algorithm (GA), with an improved version (IVMA) for problems with massive duplicate data. Through real cases of organizational assessment and guava production, VMA/IVMA outperforms traditional algorithms like PSO, Bi-PSO, GA, and GA-PSO in both solution quality and computational speed, verifying MAM’s universality and effectiveness.

  2. Huang, Y. J., Huo, S.*, & Dong, Y. (2025). Exploring the role of AI in smart grids: a 20-year bibliometric-based overview. International Journal of General Systems, 1–32. DOI: 10.1080/03081079.2025.2547187 (Q2 SCI)
    3. This study presents a 20-year (2005–2024) bibliometric and qualitative analysis of 1,272 Web of Science publications to explore AI’s evolving role in smart grids, covering macro trends (publication growth, key institutions/authors) and micro thematic patterns. It identifies eight core research clusters (e.g., load management, cybersecurity, grid optimization) and applies a Research Potential Evaluation model to assess the maturity and emerging value of these clusters. The findings offer a comprehensive framework for understanding the current state of AI-smart grid integration and guide future research directions, especially in high-potential areas like AI-driven load forecasting and predictive maintenance.

    5. This study conducts a scientometric analysis of pandemic-triggered emergency supply chain management innovations, using bibliometrics, Dynamic Topic Models (DTM), and regression analysis on 729 WOS-core articles.   5. Huang, Y. J.(Presenter), & Huo, S. C.(2025). Urban mobility demand forecasting with a hybrid attention-XGBoost model: A case study on NYC taxi data. Accepted for publication in the Proceedings of the 9th IET Smart Cities Symposium (IET SCS 2025), Bahrain.
    6. This paper proposes a hybrid Attention-XGBoost model—integrating a mutual information-based attention mechanism for feature weighting with the XGBoost gradient-boosted tree model—to predict hourly taxi demand in New York City (NYC). Using NYC yellow taxi trip data (Jan 2024–Mar 2025) and 22-dimensional features (e.g., 12-hour historical demand lags, cyclic temporal encodings), the model outperforms standard XGBoost and LSTM baselines on the Mar 2025 validation set, achieving lower MAE/RMSE and ~92% R². The model supports smart city mobility management (e.g., proactive taxi supply adjustment) and offers interpretability via attention weights, with extendability to other cities and mobility services.

  6. Li, X. L., Huo, S. C.(Presenter), & Huang, Y. J.(2025). Adaptive urban congestion modeling and zonal signal control based on taxi trajectory data. Accepted for publication in the Proceedings of the 9th IET Smart Cities Symposium (IET SCS 2025), Bahrain.
    7. This study proposes a regionalized urban traffic signal control strategy integrating taxi trajectory data mining, OD heatmap clustering, and congestion index modeling to address peak-hour congestion, as traditional static timing methods lack real-time adaptability. Using New York City 2025 Yellow Taxi peak-hour data, it constructs OD heat networks, clusters zones via K-Means, defines a "congestion pressure index", and designs a three-tier responsive signal mechanism. Minute-level simulations verify the strategy’s high deployability—alleviating peak local congestion, enhancing system stability, and offering a data-driven solution for intelligent traffic signal management.
